About the Forum

The EAP Energy Knowledge and Learning Forum is the first structured regional platform for peer learning on the sustainable energy transition in East Asia and the Pacific.

Convened by the World Bank Group with support from ESMAP and international partners including the International Energy Agency (IEA), the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, the Forum brings together approximately 100 participants — Ministers of Energy, CEOs and technical managers from power utilities, World Bank management and staff, and representatives from IFC and MIGA.

Over three days, participants will engage in high-level ministerial dialogue, technical sessions, and parallel sessions organized around six core themes: variable renewable energy integration and grid stability; regional power grid and market integration; utility digitalization and performance improvement; innovative financing for energy infrastructure; last-mile and off-grid electrification in the Pacific islands; and industrial decarbonization.

A central feature of the Forum will be the formal launch of Communities of Practice — designed to sustain peer exchange and technical collaboration well beyond Bali.

What participants will take home

  • Actionable knowledge — case studies and operational lessons participants can apply Monday morning.
  • Stronger regional networks — direct connections to peers grappling with the same challenges.
  • A shared foundation for accelerating the energy transition — informing national policy reforms, enhancing implementation of ongoing energy programs, and strengthening the technical capacity of government officials.

Key messages

A region on the move, a forum built for action

The Forum marks the shift from high-level ambition to practical delivery, grounded in the real experiences of countries already implementing energy programs across the region.

Global expertise, regional ownership

Backed by the World Bank Academy, ESMAP, MIGA, IFC and international partners including the IEA, IRENA, and Berkeley National Laboratory, the Forum connects EAP countries to the best available global knowledge on renewable energy integration, grid modernization, regional power trade and industrial decarbonization — while keeping the focus firmly on locally relevant, actionable solutions.
