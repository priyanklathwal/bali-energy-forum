About the Forum

Convened by the World Bank Group with support from ESMAP and international partners — the Forum is the first structured regional platform for peer learning on the sustainable energy transition in East Asia and the Pacific.

Over three days, approximately 100 participants — Ministers of Energy, CEOs and technical managers from power utilities, World Bank management and staff, and representatives from IFC and MIGA — will engage in high-level ministerial dialogue, technical sessions, and parallel sessions organized around six core themes.

A central feature of the Forum will be the formal launch of Communities of Practice, designed to sustain peer exchange and technical collaboration well beyond Bali.

Why this Forum, why now

Global expertise, regional ownership

Backed by the World Bank Academy, ESMAP, MIGA, IFC and international partners including the IEA, IRENA, and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, the Forum connects EAP countries to the best available global knowledge — while keeping the focus firmly on locally relevant, actionable solutions that countries can take home and implement.

From ambition to delivery

Recent oil market volatility has sharpened the urgency of reducing dependence on imported fossil fuels. The transition to domestic renewable energy is increasingly a matter of national energy security. The Forum addresses the policy and regulatory questions Ministries and utilities are grappling with right now — from enabling variable renewables at scale, to designing frameworks for regional power trade, to attracting the financing the transition requires.

Knowledge that keeps going

Beyond Bali, the Communities of Practice connect participants to a permanent regional network of utility professionals, grid operators, and technical experts — ensuring the relationships and knowledge built in Bali continue to grow long after the Forum closes.
