About the Forum

The first regional platform for peer learning on the sustainable energy transition in East Asia and the Pacific.

Convened by the World Bank Group with support from the Energy Sector Management Assistance Program (ESMAP), International Energy Agency (IEA), International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. The Forum brings together government representatives, utility leadership, and energy sector stakeholders from across the region.

EAP’s Energy Context

Rising demand, fossil dependence, and a sharper focus on energy security.

A Central Feature

Communities of Practice that keep the conversation going beyond Bali.

The formal launch of regional Communities of Practice is designed to sustain peer exchange and technical collaboration long after the Forum closes. The CoPs connect participants to a permanent network of utility professionals, grid operators, and technical experts, organized around the themes that matter most to their work.
