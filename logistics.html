Visa & Entry to Indonesia

Indonesia’s entry requirements vary by nationality. Most Forum participants will fall into one of the categories below — please confirm against the official guidance for your passport.

Visa-on-arrival eligible Citizens of many ASEAN, OECD and Pacific countries can obtain a Visa on Arrival (B1 / B211A) or use the eVOA portal at molina.imigrasi.go.id. Validity: typically 30 days, extendable once.
Visa-free entry Citizens of several ASEAN states may enter visa-free for short visits. Verify on the Directorate General of Immigration site before travel.
Pre-arranged visa required If your passport does not qualify for VoA, apply for a B1 / B211A visa through your nearest Indonesian Embassy or Consulate at least 4–6 weeks before travel.
World Bank participants If you hold a UN Laissez-Passer or are travelling on official Bank business, confirm any country-specific letter requirements with your unit’s administrative team. A facilitation letter from the World Bank Jakarta office can be requested via the contacts below.
Documents to carry Passport valid ≥6 months beyond your travel dates · return/onward ticket · proof of accommodation (Sofitel reservation confirmation) · invitation letter (issued separately by the EAP Energy team).

Health & safety

  • Travel insurance is strongly recommended. World Bank participants should follow Bank travel-insurance policies.
  • Vaccinations: no specific vaccinations are mandatory for entry to Indonesia from most countries; standard travel vaccinations are advised.
  • Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Cards widely accepted; ATMs available at the hotel.
  • Time zone: WITA (UTC+8).
  • Climate: June is dry season in Bali; expect 24–31°C with low humidity.
