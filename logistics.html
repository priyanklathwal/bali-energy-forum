Travel documentation

Please make sure you have the following documents upon arrival at the port of entry in Indonesia:

  1. Valid passport — valid for at least 6 months beyond your date of entry, with at least one blank page for a stamp.
  2. Visa — obtain the appropriate visa for your nationality (see the Visa section below). The World Bank can provide invitation letters and supporting documents on request.
  3. All Indonesia Digital Declaration — complete online at allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id or via the mobile app (Google Play / App Store) up to 3 days before arrival. Save the QR code for immigration and customs checkpoints.
  4. Return / onward ticket — proof of return or onward travel.
  5. Accommodation confirmation — Sofitel reservation confirmation, or a confirmed address of stay in Bali.

Visa & Entry to Indonesia

Each participant is personally responsible for obtaining the necessary visa. The World Bank can assist by providing official invitation letters or supporting documents — see World Bank support below.

For non-speaking participants

Indonesia’s entry requirements vary by nationality. Three main categories:

a) Visa-free entry (up to 30 days)

Citizens of the following states may enter without a visa for stays of up to 30 days:

ASEAN: Brunei Darussalam, Cambodia, Lao PDR, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Timor-Leste, Viet Nam.
Other: Brazil, Colombia, Hong Kong SAR, Suriname, Türkiye.

b) Visa on Arrival (VoA) or e-VoA — B1 visa

  • Eligible countries: imigrasi.go.id/wna/daftar-negara-voa-bvk-calling-visa
  • Applicable type: B1 Visa — for visitors attending seminars.
  • How to obtain: Available at designated international airports and seaports, including Bali’s Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) and Jakarta’s Soekarno-Hatta. Apply online in advance at evisa.imigrasi.go.id to avoid queues (strongly recommended).
  • Further details: B1 visa page

c) Visa required in advance — C1 visa

  • Nationals of countries not covered above must apply online or via an Indonesian Embassy/Consulate before travelling.
  • Most participants in this category will obtain the C1 Tourism Visitor Visa: C1 visa page
  • Processing time: approximately 7–10 working days.

For speakers — C10 visa (required regardless of nationality)

Indonesia requires all speakers at the Forum to obtain the C10 Business Events Speaker Visitor Visa, regardless of nationality.

  • Further details: C10 visa page
  • Processing time: approximately 7–10 working days.

General entry requirements (all travelers)

  • Passport validity ≥6 months beyond your date of entry, with one blank page.
  • Return / onward ticket.
  • Accommodation confirmation.
  • All Indonesia Digital Declaration — complete up to 3 days before arrival at allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id. Save the QR code.

C. Local Insights & Practical Information

Voltage & electricity

Voltage220–230V
Frequency50 Hz
Plug typesType C and Type F — two round pins, European style
OutletsWidely available in hotels, conference venues, and airports.
RecommendationBring a universal travel adapter. Travelers from North America, Japan, or other 110–120V regions should check device labels — a voltage converter may be required.

Weather

June falls within Bali’s dry season — low rainfall, moderate humidity, strong tropical sun, particularly between 11:00 AM and 3:00 PM.

Daytime28–30°C (82–86°F)
Nighttime24–26°C (75–79°F)

Currency

  • Local currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR).
  • As of late April 2026, the exchange rate is approximately USD 1 = IDR 16,900–17,335 (rates may fluctuate).
  • Currency exchange services and ATMs are readily available at the airport, hotels, and nearby commercial areas.
  • International participants should notify their banks before travel to avoid card-use disruptions.

Mobile phone & payments

Mobile service

  • Your home cell service may activate roaming on arrival — check your operator’s rates in advance.
  • A local prepaid SIM or eSIM is a practical alternative. Main operators: Telkomsel (widest coverage), XL Axiata, Indosat/IM3, and Tri. Buy at the airport, operator outlets, convenience stores, or malls (passport required).
  • Free Wi-Fi is available at the airport and the hotel.

Payments

  • Mixed cash and cashless ecosystem. Credit and debit cards widely accepted at hotels, conference venues, and most Nusa Dua restaurants. Carry some cash too for convenience.
  • Digital wallets in common use: GoPay, OVO, DANA, ShopeePay. Some require a local phone number for verification.
  • Most merchants accept payments via QRIS, Indonesia’s unified QR code system.

Safety & security

Indonesia maintains a stable security environment, with no nationwide concerns expected during early June. Bali is one of Indonesia’s most secure and well-prepared destinations for international conferences. Security arrangements are in place around tourist areas, hotels, and conference venues; police presence around major venues is visible and proactive. Indonesia is naturally exposed to earthquakes and volcanic activity, but no significant natural hazard alerts currently affect Bali. Participants can expect a safe environment throughout the Forum.

Practical guidance

  • Keep valuables secure; use hotel safes; avoid displaying phones near roads.
  • Be vigilant in busy public areas and at night.
  • Use licensed taxis, hotel transport, or ride-hailing (Grab / Gojek).
  • Avoid scooter rental unless experienced and insured.
  • Drink bottled water; follow hygiene precautions; avoid contact with stray animals (rabies risk).
  • Follow hotel and local authority guidance during severe weather.

Nearby attractions

Beaches

  • Nusa Dua Beach — calm waters, white sand, ideal for swimming.
  • Geger Beach (Pantai Geger) — quieter public beach with gentle waves, cafés, walking paths.
  • Water Blow Nusa Dua — dramatic coastal viewpoint, best at high tide.

Culture, arts & local experiences

  • Devdan Show at Bali Nusa Dua Theatre — cultural performance showcasing Indonesia’s traditions.
  • Museum PASIFIKA — one of Southeast Asia’s finest art museums, focused on Asia-Pacific.
  • Puja Mandala Worship Complex — five major places of worship in one site.

Short excursions (15–30 minutes from Nusa Dua)

  • Garuda Wisnu Kencana (GWK) Cultural Park — iconic monumental statue and cultural park.
  • Pandawa Beach — cliff-framed beach with kayaking and photo spots.
  • Gunung Payung Beach — scenic, less-crowded beach via cliff steps.

Activities & leisure

  • Tanjung Benoa — hub for water sports (parasailing, jet ski, banana boat, snorkeling).
  • Bali National Golf Club — championship course near Nusa Dua.
  • Nusa Dua Promenade — well-maintained coastal walking/cycling path.

Dining & light shopping

  • Bali Collection — open-air shopping and dining complex.
