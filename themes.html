Six Core Themes

Discussion at the Forum is organized around six themes that reflect the practical questions Ministries and utilities across East Asia and the Pacific are grappling with right now, from enabling variable renewables at scale, to designing frameworks for regional power trade, to attracting financing for the energy transition.

1. Variable Renewable Energy Integration & Grid Stability

Scaling up renewables in EAP, how the region compares globally and what really works. Country cases from China, the Philippines, Indonesia (ISLE / PLN), and Lao–Viet Nam (Monsoon Wind).

Day 1, Session 1 ›

2. Regional Power Grid & Market Integration

The ASEAN Power Grid vision and the Enhanced APG MoU. Financing cross-border interconnections, the APGF role, and the Global Power Pool Playbook. Subsea cables and operational experience from KPX.

Day 1, Session 2 · Day 2, Session B1

3. Utility Digitalization & Performance Improvement

The VRE-Ready Utility of the Future. KEPCO’s benchmark, NSMO’s transmission digitalization, Tata Power Delhi’s distribution turnaround, and PLN’s smart, solar-ready grid.

Day 2, Session A1 ›

4. Innovative Financing for Energy Infrastructure

MDB innovation, IFC and MIGA platforms, climate finance (CIF, GEF), and utility-level cases like PLN. Financial intermediaries for industrial-park decarbonization, Huaxia Bank, ICDF, VNEEF, EITF.

Day 2, Session A2 & B2 ›

5. Last-Mile & Off-Grid Electrification (Pacific Islands)

Reaching the unserved with cost-effective technology in Small Island Developing States. Cases from RMI, Indonesian outer islands (PLN), Philippines, Fiji and FSM.

Day 2, Session 4 ›

6. Industrial Decarbonization

Global tech and policy trends in the EAP context. Country programs from Indonesia, Viet Nam and China, plus an interactive breakout on energy–industry synergy led by UC Berkeley.

Day 1, Session 3 ›

Why these six?

The six themes mirror the three Focus Business Areas of the EAP Energy practice, renewable energy at scale, regional grid integration, and industrial decarbonization, with utility performance, off-grid electrification, and innovative finance as the cross-cutting enablers that turn ambition into delivery.

For utility and technical teams: The Forum is designed to surface operational knowledge you can use on Monday morning, hands-on case studies and lessons drawn from practitioners who have built and run these systems in contexts similar to yours.
