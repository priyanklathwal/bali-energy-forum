Six Core Themes

Discussion at the Forum is organized around six themes that reflect the practical questions ministries and utilities across East Asia and the Pacific are grappling with right now: from enabling variable renewables at scale, to designing frameworks for regional power trade, to attracting financing for the energy transition.

1. Variable Renewable Energy Integration and Grid Stability

Scaling up renewables in East Asia and the Pacific, how the region compares globally, and what really works. Country cases from China, the Philippines, Indonesia (the ISLE program with Perusahaan Listrik Negara), and the Lao PDR–Viet Nam Monsoon Wind project.

Day 1, Session 1 ›

2. Regional Power Grid and Market Integration

The ASEAN Power Grid vision and the Enhanced Memorandum of Understanding. Financing cross-border interconnections, the role of the ASEAN Power Grid Financing initiative, and the Global Power Pool Playbook. Subsea cables and operational experience from the Korea Power Exchange.

Day 1, Session 2 · Day 2, Session B1

3. Utility Digitalization and Performance Improvement

The renewable-ready utility of the future. Korea Electric Power Corporation’s benchmark, the National System and Market Operator of Viet Nam’s transmission digitalization, Tata Power Delhi’s distribution turnaround, and Perusahaan Listrik Negara’s smart, solar-ready grid.

Day 2, Session A1 ›

4. Innovative Financing for Energy Infrastructure

Multilateral development bank innovation, International Finance Corporation and Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency platforms, climate finance through the Climate Investment Funds and the Global Environment Facility, and utility-level cases. Financial intermediaries for industrial-park decarbonization, including Huaxia Bank and the country-level funds from Indonesia, Viet Nam, and the Philippines.

Day 2, Sessions A2 and B2 ›

5. Last-Mile and Off-Grid Electrification (Pacific Islands)

Reaching the unserved with cost-effective technology in Small Island Developing States. Cases from the Republic of the Marshall Islands, the Indonesian outer islands, the Philippines, Fiji, and the Federated States of Micronesia.

Day 2, Session 4 ›

6. Industrial Decarbonization

Global technology and policy trends in the East Asia and Pacific context. Country programs from Indonesia, Viet Nam, and China, plus an interactive breakout on energy–industry synergy led by the University of California, Berkeley.

Day 1, Session 3 ›

Why these six?

The six themes mirror the three Focus Business Areas of the East Asia and Pacific Energy practice — renewable energy at scale, regional grid integration, and industrial decarbonization — with utility performance, off-grid electrification, and innovative finance as the cross-cutting enablers that turn ambition into delivery.

For utility and technical teams: The Forum is designed to surface operational, ready-to-apply knowledge — hands-on case studies and lessons drawn from practitioners who have built and run these systems in contexts similar to yours.
