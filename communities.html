Communities of Practice

A central feature of the Forum is the formal launch of Communities of Practice (CoPs) — designed to sustain peer exchange and technical collaboration well beyond Bali.

The CoPs connect participants to a permanent regional network of utility professionals, grid operators, and technical experts organized around the themes that matter most to their work. Backed by ESMAP, IFC, MIGA and the World Bank’s Knowledge team, the CoPs ensure the relationships and knowledge built in Bali continue to grow — giving teams ongoing access to peer expertise and global technical support long after the Forum.

Priority CoP themes

ASEAN Power Grid

Regional power trade, cross-border interconnection, market integration, system operations.

Renewable Energy

VRE integration, grid stability, scaling solar and wind, storage and flexibility.

Industrial Decarbonization

Sector strategies, energy–industry synergy, financing mechanisms and emerging areas including green hydrogen.

Join an ASET Community of Practice that keeps the conversation going beyond the Forum. The CoP launch session on Day 2 (15:15–16:30) will identify priority themes and champions, and capture interest through a start-up survey.

Why Communities of Practice?

  • Continuity — turning a three-day event into a long-running peer network.
  • Operational depth — case studies and lessons from practitioners who have built and run these systems in contexts similar to yours.
  • Access — ongoing connection to global technical expertise via ESMAP, IFC, MIGA, IEA, IRENA and Berkeley Lab.
  • Influence — communities of practice that can inform regional initiatives such as APGF, ASEAN-led work, and Pacific energy programs.

How to engage

Participants are invited to nominate themselves as champions for one of the three priority CoP themes during the Day 2 launch session. Further engagement details — including how to join, suggested cadence, and the secretariat support model — will be shared with registered participants on the Logistics page.
